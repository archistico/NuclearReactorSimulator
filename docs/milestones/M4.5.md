# M4.5 — Generator, Grid & Synchronization Physics

## Goal

Connect the validated M4.2 turbine rotor domain to an explicit deterministic electrical domain suitable for manual synchronization and shaft-to-grid energy accounting.

## Delivered

- strongly typed `Frequency`, `ElectricPotential`, `PhaseAngle` and `PhaseAngleDifference` quantities;
- `ElectricalGridDefinition`, `SynchronousGeneratorDefinition` and `GeneratorGridSystemDefinition` over the validated M4.4 condensate/feedwater system;
- exact one-generator-per-turbine-rotor topology validation;
- explicit `GeneratorGridState` with deterministic grid/generator phase and breaker state;
- generator frequency derived from rotor speed and pole-pair count;
- manual breaker close/open commands with explicit frequency/phase/voltage synchronization windows;
- mandatory zeroing of the legacy M4.2 manual load-torque seam while M4.5 owns rotor loading;
- deterministic requested electrical-power to electromagnetic-torque feedback through the existing M4.2 rotor integrator;
- explicit shaft-to-electrical conversion efficiency and loss accounting;
- `GeneratorElectricalAudit` plus generator/grid snapshots;
- higher-phase supplemental thermofluid composition preserved through the wrapped full M4.4 → M4.3 → M4.2 stack;
- topology, quantity, synchronization, breaker, load-seam, audit and determinism tests;
- ADR 0036 and `docs/GENERATOR_GRID_SYNCHRONIZATION.md`.

## Explicitly deferred

- AVR/excitation dynamics;
- turbine governor and automatic load control;
- automatic synchronizer;
- electrical protection/interlocks;
- detailed synchronous-machine transient model;
- full integrated secondary-cycle energy balance.

## Validation

Validated after successful local build and complete automated test-suite execution reported by the user.

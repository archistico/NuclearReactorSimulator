# M1.6 — Heat Transfer

## Goal

Introduce deterministic conservative heat transfer, lumped thermal inertia and explicit external heat sources without implementing the water/steam phase model.

## Scope

- strongly typed `HeatCapacity` and `ThermalConductance`;
- immutable lumped thermal-body definitions/states;
- conserved stored thermal energy with derived temperature;
- passive bidirectional heat transfer from signed temperature difference;
- equal-and-opposite endpoint thermal balances;
- deterministic thermal-body integration;
- explicit external heat-source energy input;
- coupling of thermal power into existing fluid-node energy balances;
- fixed-step/pulse-segmentation determinism tests.

## Acceptance criteria

- [x] no implicit `double` unit boundaries added;
- [x] internal thermal links conserve energy by construction;
- [x] heat flow reverses when temperature gradient reverses;
- [x] equal temperatures produce exactly zero heat flow;
- [x] thermal-body temperature derives from conserved energy and heat capacity;
- [x] invalid below-absolute-zero candidate energy fails before commit;
- [x] external heat-source energy is explicitly accounted;
- [x] wall-to-fluid energy transfer composes with `FluidNodeBalance`;
- [x] fixed-step runtime remains pulse-segmentation deterministic;
- [x] no water/steam phase closure introduced prematurely.

## Out of scope

- water/steam saturation and phase equilibrium;
- enthalpy/steam quality;
- boiling and condensation correlations;
- critical heat flux;
- spatial conduction meshes;
- reactor fuel/cladding thermal models.

# M2.4 — Thermal Power

## Goal

Introduce the explicit deterministic conversion from normalized neutron population to instantaneous fission thermal power and route that power into existing thermal/fluid energy boundaries without yet adding decay heat or thermal feedback.

## Scope

- explicit `FissionPowerCalibration` between reference neutron population and reference thermal power;
- validated heat-deposition fractions and canonical destination definitions;
- stateless deterministic `FissionPowerSolver`;
- exact total-power closure across configured heat destinations;
- immutable `FissionPowerSnapshot` diagnostics;
- adapters to `ThermalEnergyBalance` and zero-mass `FluidNodeBalance`;
- integration with validated thermal-body/fluid-node energy integrators;
- composition with M2.3 point kinetics in the deterministic runtime;
- fail-fast handling of non-finite/overflowed fission-power scaling.

## Non-goals

- decay heat inventory or post-scram thermal tail;
- fuel/coolant temperature reactivity feedback;
- void feedback;
- spatial neutron flux or core power shape;
- channel-wise RBMK power distribution;
- plant-specific nominal power constants hardcoded in the engine.

## Acceptance

- reference neutron population produces reference fission thermal power;
- zero neutron population produces zero fission thermal power;
- thermal power scales linearly with normalized neutron population;
- configured destination powers close exactly to total fission thermal power;
- thermal deposition creates energy but never fluid mass;
- total integrated deposited energy equals integrated fission thermal power for the closed deposition partition;
- M2.3 kinetics → M2.4 power → thermal/fluid integration remains deterministic under external pulse segmentation;
- invalid calibration/partition definitions and numerical overflow fail fast;
- build and complete test suite pass locally.

# M7.4 — Heat-Up, Steam Raising & Turbine Startup

## Objective

Provide the first guided progression from a stable low-power reactor condition through primary heat-up, usable steam conditions and turbine roll/acceleration, while preserving canonical M2–M5 physics ownership and keeping grid synchronization, breaker closure and electrical loading outside the milestone.

## Delivered

1. Exact built-in initial-condition identity `low-power-steam-raising` version `1`.
2. `HeatUpTurbineStartupInitialConditionFactory` reusing the validated M7.2 canonical construction path rather than duplicating the M1–M5 runtime object graph.
3. Warm critical low-power seed with main circulation established, control rods at the zero-worth midpoint, modeled steam pressure available, turbine stopped and generator breaker open.
4. Versioned turbine-startup lineup with stop/admission availability and the governing control valve initially closed. This is initial-condition data; M7.4 does not invent a new direct stop-valve operator owner.
5. M7.4 scenario permissions enabling reactor trim/safety, MCP commands and turbine-speed raise/lower while continuing to reject generator-breaker close and generator-load raise/lower.
6. `HeatUpTurbineStartupChecklistEvaluator` observing only immutable `ControlRoomSnapshot` data for signal health, protection, circulation, heating power, drum pressure/inventory, startup lineup, turbine speed and generator isolation.
7. Ordered declarative guidance for hot handoff verification, heat-up/steam raising, startup-lineup verification, turbine roll, warm-up/acceleration and the M7.5 synchronization handoff.
8. Desktop composition loading the exact M7.4 session paused through the validated M7.1 registry/session boundary.
9. Application tests for exact-version reconstruction, startup-lineup state, action gating, initial checklist state and the validated turbine-speed controller seam opening the governing valve.
10. ADR 0056 and `docs/HEAT_UP_STEAM_RAISING_TURBINE_STARTUP.md`.

## Non-goals

- no direct UI/scenario ownership of stop-valve physics;
- no forced heat-up, pressure correction or inventory trimming;
- no generator synchronization procedure;
- no generator-breaker closure;
- no electrical load increase;
- no automatic predetermined startup trajectory;
- no fault injection or hidden randomness;
- no duplicate physical state owner.

- generator mechanical input;
- requested electrical power;
- actual electrical output;
- synchronization state;
- breaker state;
- turbine/generator protection state.

## Why MWe can fall to zero

The synchronous-generator model does not create electrical power merely because the rotor is spinning. When paralleled, requested electrical load produces electromagnetic load torque. Actual electrical output is derived from the mechanical power transferred by the rotor to that load. If sustained steam/turbine shaft input is insufficient, rotor kinetic energy can be consumed, speed can decay, and electrical output can eventually collapse.

Therefore a 0 MWe observation is not automatically an operator mistake. It can indicate an incomplete operating sequence, a trip/protection condition, insufficient steam/shaft support, or an integrated seed/balance regression. M10.9.4 adds explicit diagnostics and long-running acceptance tests to distinguish these cases.

## Hotfix 8 — continuous main-steam supply gradient

The repeated Hotfix 6/7 `exhaust` depletion was traced to the upstream initialization, not to the condenser law. In v2, `steam` and `header` were both initialized at 280 °C saturated conditions, so the canonical `main-steam-line` pressure difference was zero and its initial flow was 0 kg/s. Downstream valves nevertheless had a pressure staircase and could initially feed the turbine by draining only the finite preloaded `header` / `stop-out` / `control-out` / `turbine-inlet` inventories. Once those buffers collapsed, turbine exhaust replenishment disappeared while condensation continued.

Hotfix 8 adds a backward-compatible optional header-steam initialization temperature and uses it only in the v2 generation-ready profiles. The v2 staged saturation temperatures are now 280 / 275 / 269.5 / 253 / 246 °C from drum steam through turbine inlet. With the existing 2,800 Pa·s²/kg² v2 resistances and 61% control-valve bias, each canonical segment begins near 13 kg/s, establishing continuous upstream replenishment instead of a finite downstream reservoir. Historical v1 factories retain the default `header = primary steam` behavior exactly.

Ordinary v2 seed regressions now require both >10 kg/s main-steam-line flow and a positive source-to-header pressure difference before the seed can be considered generation-ready. No canonical M4 solver or replay identity is changed.


## Hotfix 9 — generation-scale condenser steam-space

Hotfix 8 proved that a continuous upstream main-steam gradient alone was insufficient: the v2 condenser `exhaust` control volume still inherited the generic historical 10 m³ node size. At approximately 40 °C that represents only about half a kilogram of near-dry saturated steam, while the generation-ready turbine moves on the order of 13 kg/s. A single 0.01 s deterministic step therefore transfers a material fraction of the entire exhaust inventory, making the node excessively sensitive to small instantaneous mismatches between turbine inflow and condensation.

Hotfix 9 adds a backward-compatible optional exhaust steam-space volume to the operational-seed factory. Historical callers and all v1 seeds retain exactly 10 m³; only the versioned v2 desktop and synchronization profiles use 1,000 m³. Connectivity, solver ownership and thermodynamic laws remain unchanged. At that checkpoint the v2 path still used 1,800 Pa·s²/kg² steam/admission resistances and 24.5 MW condenser cooling; Hotfix 10 subsequently increases hydraulic control authority while preserving the larger steam-space buffer.

The ordinary v2 seed regression now asserts explicit stage flow and rotor shaft-power ranges before long-running acceptance is attempted. M10.9.4 remains NOT VALIDATED until the ordinary suite and explicit long-gameplay pack both pass locally.

## Hotfix 13 — pressure-driven turbine expansion hydraulic closure

Hotfix 13 is deliberately rebased on Hotfix 10, the last candidate reported with a clean build and ordinary suite. The unvalidated Hotfix 11 condenser-flow-following workaround and Hotfix 12 speculative fuel/structure thermal closure are withdrawn from the current branch so the structural turbine defect can be tested in isolation.

The root cause is algebraic. The canonical valve network already transfers mass through stop → control → admission into `turbine-inlet`, while both M5.4 and M5.5 derived turbine stage drain as `min(F_stop, F_control, F_admission)`. Therefore `d(m_stop-out + m_control-out + m_turbine-inlet)/dt = F_stop - F_stage >= 0`; the admission train is a monotonic accumulator and pressure equalization/zero flow is the inevitable terminal state.

`TurbineStageGroupDefinition` now has optional `ExpansionResistance`. Null preserves the historical law only for explicit legacy definitions. Current v2 desktop and synchronization definitions use 21,400 Pa·s²/kg². A single shared `TurbineStageMassFlowResolver` replaces the duplicated M5.4/M5.5 implementations. For current v2 it computes `sqrt((P_inlet - P_exhaust)/R)`, blocks reverse flow and limits one fixed-step drain to half of committed inlet mass. The existing `TurbineExpansionSolver` source terms remain the authoritative mass/energy transfer from inlet to exhaust.

An ordinary 200-step regression asserts that combined stop/control/inlet inventory remains within ±5% and that settled stage flow agrees with admission-valve flow. This gate is intentionally short and structural; the separate explicit 60-second gameplay journeys remain the end-to-end acceptance test.

Hotfix 13 also adopts the policy that legacy replay compatibility must not constrain current-model correctness. Exact pre-release legacy replay may be isolated/migrated/deprecated when required rather than forcing a known physical defect into the active model. M10.9.4 remains NOT VALIDATED until both ordinary and explicit long-running gates pass locally.


## Hotfix 14 — turbine hydraulic invariant regression contract

Hotfix 14 keeps the Hotfix 13 pressure-driven expansion implementation unchanged and corrects the short structural test contract. The prior final assertion required instantaneous equality between admission-valve flow and turbine-stage flow. That equality is not a transient invariant because `turbine-inlet` is a compressible inventory: `F_admission - F_stage` is precisely the local mass-storage term. The observed 14.557 versus 13.448 kg/s therefore does not by itself indicate recurrence of the old ratchet.

The regression now retains the physically decisive combined-train final inventory bound (±5%), samples the 200-step trajectory and requires at least one negative inventory increment, directly disproving the historical non-decreasing train invariant. Admission and stage flows must remain finite and positive/in-range. No production physics law changes in Hotfix 14.

The wider structural audit is recorded in `STRUCTURAL_PLANT_MODEL_STABILIZATION_PLAN.md`; follow-on corrections must be isolated one at a time, beginning with condenser pressure/heat-transfer feedback only after this turbine hydraulic gate and long-gameplay evidence are evaluated.

## Hotfix 15 — steam-drum inventory closure

Hotfix 14 compiled and the complete ordinary suite passed. The explicit 60-second journeys then failed in the canonical `drum` thermodynamic node rather than in the turbine admission train. Algebraic audit found a second structural accumulator.

Historical M3.6 separation emitted `F_steam = x*F_return` and `F_liquid = F_return-F_steam`, so separator drain was exactly `F_return`. Because the canonical return pipe already adds `+F_return` and M4.4 adds physical feedwater through the feedwater pump, the closed-cycle drum balance reduced to `dm_drum/dt = F_feedwater >= 0`. The drum therefore had no structural path to shed feedwater inventory.

Hotfix 15 adds explicit legacy/current liquid-recirculation semantics. Current v2 uses committed positive MCP demand for the liquid recirculation sink, while steam remains a separate drain to the steam outlet. The current balance becomes `F_return + F_feedwater - F_MCP - F_steam`, removing the monotonic ratchet while keeping `PlantNetworkOrchestrator` as the single integrator. See ADR 0082.

This correction is intentionally not a claim that the complete source side is now final. Pressure/heat-driven boiling, steam-dump/safety-valve behavior and detailed drum swell/shrink remain separate structural/fidelity items.

## Hotfix 17 — Condenser UA·ΔT Pressure Feedback

Hotfix 17 advances the next structural stabilization item without mixing generator, pump, protection or integrator changes.

Current-v2 condensers now define a positive overall heat-transfer conductance and cooling-boundary inputs include coolant temperature. The canonical M4.3 thermal capacity is:

```text
Qsurface = UA * max(0, Tsteam - Tcoolant)
Qeffective = min(Qavailable, Qsurface)
```

The existing 24.5 MW / 40 °C initial condenser point is preserved with 20 °C cooling water and `UA = 1.225 MW/K`. As condenser steam temperature falls toward cooling-water temperature, heat rejection and condensation now weaken automatically; at non-positive ΔT the surface-transfer contribution is zero.

Direct regressions cover the UA limit, monotonic weakening with ΔT, zero transfer at non-positive ΔT and legacy null-UA isolation. ADR 0084 records the decision. The user confirmed compilation, the ordinary suite and both explicit 60-second gameplay journeys are green on Hotfix 17; it is the validated structural base for Hotfix 18.

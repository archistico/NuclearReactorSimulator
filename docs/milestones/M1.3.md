# M1.3 — Pipes & Flow Resistance

## Goal

Connect two validated M1.2 fluid nodes with a deterministic passive bidirectional flow model that converts pressure difference into conservative mass and energy balances.

## Included

- strongly typed `QuadraticHydraulicResistance` with canonical SI storage;
- immutable `PipeDefinition` with stable reference endpoints;
- stateless bidirectional `PipeFlowSolver`;
- quadratic pressure-loss relation with natural flow reversal;
- upstream specific-internal-energy advection;
- exactly equal-and-opposite endpoint `FluidNodeBalance` values;
- endpoint identity validation;
- deterministic composition with `FluidNodeIntegrator` and fixed-step runtime;
- automated tests for direction, reversal, zero differential, energy advection, conservation and pulse segmentation;
- ADR 0010 and `docs/PIPES_AND_FLOW.md`.

## Explicitly excluded

- valves;
- pumps;
- active pressure head;
- pipe inventory/inertia;
- detailed geometry/friction correlations;
- elevation head;
- two-phase pressure drop;
- final enthalpy/phase transport.

## Acceptance

```powershell
dotnet restore
dotnet build --no-restore
dotnet test --no-build
```

Expected properties:

- all existing M0–M1.2.1 tests remain green;
- high `FromNode` pressure produces positive reference flow;
- reversed pressure difference reverses flow without changing pipe identity;
- equal pressures produce exactly zero transfer;
- endpoint mass and internal-energy balances sum exactly to zero;
- a two-node pipe step conserves total mass and internal energy;
- pulse segmentation does not change fixed-step runtime results.

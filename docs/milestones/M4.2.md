# M4.2 — Turbine Rotor & Expansion Model

## Status

**VALIDATED** — local build and complete automated test-suite validation were reported successful.

## Goal

Replace the temporary M4.1 turbine-admission sink with deterministic lumped steam expansion and an explicit mechanical rotor domain, without violating the validated single committed-state / single plant-network integration contract.

## Delivered scope

- strongly typed `AngularSpeed`, `Torque` and `MomentOfInertia` quantities;
- canonical `TurbineExpansionSystemDefinition` over the validated M4.1 main-steam network;
- one lumped `TurbineStageGroupDefinition` per M4.1 turbine-admission boundary;
- explicit canonical exhaust-node target for each stage group, ready for M4.3 condenser ownership;
- simplified constant turbine efficiency and nominal specific-work characteristic;
- committed `TurbineExpansionState` with one immutable rotor state per defined rotor;
- deterministic stage-group steam mass flow, inlet energy transport, shaft-work extraction and exhaust energy transport;
- exactly conservative inlet-to-exhaust mass transfer;
- explicit thermofluid-to-shaft energy transfer in plant-network accounting;
- rotor inertia, turbine torque, external load torque, net torque and angular-speed integration;
- zero-speed-safe load-torque limiting that prevents numerical reversal without hiding the commanded load;
- mechanical energy audit for shaft power, rotor kinetic-energy change and external mechanical load;
- explicit trip command seam that blocks expansion flow without implementing automatic protection logic;
- explicit overspeed indication at committed and candidate rotor speed, without automatic trip latching;
- backward-compatible M4.1 downstream source-term composition before the existing single `PlantNetworkOrchestrator` integration;
- tests for topology, quantity behavior, conservative expansion, shaft power, rotor acceleration, trip behavior, overspeed observability and ownership of the M4.1 terminal boundary seam.

## Intentionally deferred

- condenser pressure/vacuum feedback and steam condensation;
- wet-steam erosion or detailed multi-stage turbine maps;
- moisture separation/reheat;
- bearings, turning gear and detailed mechanical losses;
- automatic overspeed protection/trip latching;
- generator electromagnetic torque, synchronization and electrical load;
- automatic turbine speed/load control.

These belong to M4.3–M5.

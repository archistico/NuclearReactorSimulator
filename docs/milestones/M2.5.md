# M2.5 — Decay Heat

## Status

Validated — local build and complete test suite passed.

## Goal

Add stateful post-fission heat generation with deterministic memory of fission-power history, without introducing isotope depletion or conflating decay heat with instantaneous M2.4 fission power.

## Delivered

- `DecayHeatGenerationFraction`;
- canonical `DecayHeatGroupDefinition` and `DecayHeatDefinition`;
- immutable latent-energy `DecayHeatGroupState` / `DecayHeatState`;
- empty and long-running equilibrium initialization;
- analytic finite-step `DecayHeatSolver`;
- explicit produced versus emitted decay-energy accounting;
- average same-step and end-of-step instantaneous decay-heat diagnostics;
- canonical complete named heat-deposition partition;
- adapters to thermal-body and fluid-node energy balances;
- fail-fast non-finite numerical envelope;
- domain, solver, integration, shutdown, half-life, conservation and runtime determinism tests;
- ADR 0019 and `docs/DECAY_HEAT.md`.

## Architectural boundary

```text
FissionPowerSolver output history
        ↓
DecayHeatSolver
        ↓
latent group energies
        ↓
emitted decay heat
        ↓
ThermalEnergyBalance / FluidNodeBalance
```

M2.5 does not modify point kinetics and does not feed back into reactivity. Temperature feedback begins in M2.6.

# M6.5 — Turbine, Generator & Electrical Panels

Status: **BASELINE CANDIDATE**

## Scope

- real Turbine & Secondary Cycle control-room workspace;
- real Generator & Electrical control-room workspace;
- measured M5.1 turbine/generator/condenser instruments with validity/quality semantics;
- explicitly labelled M4/M5 model diagnostics for non-instrumented steam-path, feedwater and electrical details;
- turbine-trip and generator-trip presentation/command intents;
- synchronization-gated breaker close/open operator intents;
- turbine-speed and generator-load raise/lower command seams for later runtime setpoint wiring;
- no simulation physics or direct authoritative-state ownership in Avalonia.

## Acceptance criteria

1. M6.4 is recorded as validated.
2. Turbine/Secondary and Electrical workspaces are real domain panels rather than shell placeholders.
3. Rotor speed, plant turbine shaft power, condenser pressure/vacuum/hotwell, generator frequency/output and gross output use measured M5.1 channels where available.
4. Non-instrumented steam, turbine, condenser/feedwater and electrical values are explicitly labelled model diagnostics.
5. Presentation topology/identity derives from canonical M4 snapshots; no synthetic equipment is invented.
6. Turbine/generator trip state observes M5.5 and command buttons emit typed Application intents only.
7. Breaker closure is disabled fail-closed when the published synchronization permissive is false or generator trip is active, while M4.5 remains the authoritative close-check owner.
8. Speed/load/breaker commands carry canonical target identity and do not directly mutate rotor, generator, valve or breaker state.
9. Avalonia continues to have no direct Simulation project/namespace dependency.
10. M6.6 Trends, Alarms & Event Timeline becomes the next milestone after validation.

Validated locally by the user: compilation and complete test suite passed. M6.5 is the validated baseline for M6.6.
